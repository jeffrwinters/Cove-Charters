# Cove Charters Product Vision

## What Cove Is

Cove Charters is a premium Lake of the Ozarks charter marketplace. It should feel upscale, trustworthy, and concierge-driven.

It is not intended to be another pontoon rental marketplace. The customer experience should focus on polished boat days, premium inventory, and guided matching.

## Website First

The customer product should be a website first, not a consumer mobile app. Most customers will likely book once per season, so an app is not needed for customers at MVP.

Back office can remain web-first. Captain workflows should eventually become app-like/mobile-first because captains will need fast access in the field. A customer app is explicitly not near-term unless usage later proves it is worth the overhead.

## Marketplace Model

Cove has multiple marketplace participants:

- Customers
- Boat owners
- Captains
- Back office operators

The system must support coordination across all of them.

## Key Product Principles

- SEO is a first-class requirement.
- Public pages should be crawlable and useful.
- Customers should be guided toward the right boat.
- The admin/back-office workflow must be efficient and clear.
- The product should support premium experiences and avoid commodity rental language.

## Legal/Operational Constraint

Because of bareboat charter laws, the customer must choose their own captain. Each boat has an approved captain list, but the customer legally makes the captain selection.

The system must maintain:

- Boat-approved captain lists
- Captain availability
- Captain acceptance
- Charter agreement signing
- Copies of agreements for captains/back office

## Back Office Persona

Back office is a primary user, not an afterthought. Back office must be able to:

- Change boats on bookings
- Change captains on bookings
- Facilitate payouts
- Track booking paid status
- Track settlements
- Manage operational closeout after trips

## Captain Persona

Captain tooling should eventually be wrapped into an app-like or mobile-first experience. Near-term, the system should keep captain-facing data clean enough to support that future surface:

- Assigned trips
- Customer and boat details
- Schedule/availability
- Agreement copies
- Trip closeout notes
- Fuel, mileage, cleaning, and damage reporting
